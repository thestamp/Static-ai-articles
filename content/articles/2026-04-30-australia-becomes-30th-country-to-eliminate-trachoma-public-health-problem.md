## Summary
The World Health Organization says Australia has eliminated trachoma as a public health problem, making it the 30th country to reach the benchmark for the preventable eye disease.

## What Happened
WHO announced the validation on 29 April 2026, crediting long-running screening, treatment and community-health efforts, including work focused on Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander communities. Australian health authorities separately described the decision as a national public-health milestone.

## Why It Matters
Trachoma is the leading infectious cause of blindness globally and is closely tied to poverty and limited access to sanitation and care. The validation is significant for science-health coverage because it highlights how sustained public-health programs can reduce preventable blindness and offers a model for other countries still pursuing elimination.
