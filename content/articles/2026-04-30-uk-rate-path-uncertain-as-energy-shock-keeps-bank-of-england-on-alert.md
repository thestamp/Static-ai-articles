The Bank of England held interest rates but warned that renewed energy-price pressure could still force tighter policy if inflation risks persist.

In a new analysis of the policy backdrop, BBC economics editor Faisal Islam reported that officials are trying to manage expectations around a broad range of outcomes tied to oil-market volatility and the wider Gulf crisis. The report says rate cuts appear off the table for now and that further increases remain possible if elevated oil prices last.

Governor Andrew Bailey said households are facing "very difficult circumstances" as fuel and food costs pressure budgets, while the central bank weighs how to prevent a broader inflation spiral. The policy signal underscores a difficult tradeoff: cushioning growth while preserving price stability if external energy shocks linger.

Why this matters for business: financing costs, mortgage pricing, and corporate borrowing assumptions could all shift quickly if energy markets stay volatile, raising uncertainty for investment and hiring decisions through the second half of the year.
