Microsoft’s AI Growth Signals Big Upside Ahead

The New York Knicks opened Game 6 with one of the most lopsided first halves in NBA playoff history, building a **47-point halftime lead** over the Atlanta Hawks before closing out the series.

## What happened
- New York's halftime margin set a new NBA postseason record for largest halftime lead.
- The Knicks converted the advantage into the biggest playoff win in franchise history and advanced to the next round.
- Atlanta never recovered from the early pace, with the game effectively decided by intermission.

## Why it matters
A result this extreme is more than a single-game anomaly: it changes scouting priorities and rotation planning for the Knicks' next opponent, while raising urgent offseason questions for Atlanta about depth, defense, and playoff readiness.

## What to watch next
- Whether New York can sustain the same defensive intensity against a stronger second-round offense.
- Injury and rest updates before the next series tips off.
- How Atlanta responds organizationally after a historically one-sided elimination game.
