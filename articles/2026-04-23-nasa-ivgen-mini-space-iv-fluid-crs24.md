# NASA Sends Mini IV-Fluid Factory to ISS on CRS-24 Mission

NASA says its new **IVGEN Mini** hardware is now aboard the International Space Station after launching on the **Northrop Grumman CRS-24** cargo mission on April 11, as the agency tests whether crews can produce medical-grade IV saline on demand during long-duration missions.

According to NASA, prepackaged IV fluid can treat a large share of in-flight medical conditions but has a shelf-life limit that becomes a constraint for multi-year deep-space plans. The agency says IVGEN Mini is designed to use station drinking water, process it through filtration and ion-removal steps, and mix sterile sodium chloride solution to generate infusion-ready saline in orbit.

Northrop Grumman's NG-24 mission profile confirms the Cygnus flight delivered the cargo package to the ISS, while NASA's mission cargo release lists biomedical and human-health payloads tied to exploration risk reduction.

Why this matters: If on-orbit IV production performs as expected, NASA could reduce both medical-supply mass and expiration risk for missions beyond low Earth orbit, where resupply windows are limited and care must be more self-sufficient.
