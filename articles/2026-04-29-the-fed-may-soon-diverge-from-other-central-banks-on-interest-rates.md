Central banks entered this week with a shared challenge—persistent inflation risk—but an increasingly unshared response on interest rates.

Recent reporting points to a widening policy gap: some major central banks are signaling patience, while others are emphasizing inflation vigilance as energy and geopolitical pressures cloud the near-term outlook.

For businesses and households, that divergence matters. Borrowing costs, currency moves, and investment timing can shift quickly when policy trajectories stop moving in lockstep.

### What to watch next

- Upcoming policy statements and inflation prints in major economies.
- Whether energy-price shocks feed into core inflation expectations.
- How markets reprice rate paths across currencies and sovereign bonds.
