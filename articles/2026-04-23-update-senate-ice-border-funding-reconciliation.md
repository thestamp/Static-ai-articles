# Update: Senate Advances ICE and Border Funding Path in Reconciliation Vote

The Senate moved forward on a partisan reconciliation track for immigration-enforcement funding, approving a budget framework that now heads to the House and could shape near-term ICE and Border Patrol appropriations.

## What’s New

- AP reports the Senate adopted a budget plan tied to reopening Department of Homeland Security funding flows and sent the framework to the House.
- NPR reports the package carries an expected **$70 billion** immigration-enforcement price tag after an overnight session.
- CBS reports Republicans advanced the resolution without Democratic support, formally launching the next reconciliation phase.

## Why this is a politics update

This is a procedural-but-consequential congressional action: it changes the legislative pathway and bargaining leverage around immigration enforcement spending.
