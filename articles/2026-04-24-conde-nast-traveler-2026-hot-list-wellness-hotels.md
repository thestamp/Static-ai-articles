# Condé Nast Traveler’s 2026 Hot List Spotlights Wellness Hotels as Global Travel Spending Climbs

Condé Nast Traveler published its 2026 Hot List package on April 23, rolling out multiple regional and thematic rankings at the same timestamp, including dedicated lists for wellness hotels, beach resorts, affordable luxury properties, and cruise ships.

For travelers, the immediate signal is practical: wellness-focused properties are no longer a niche add-on in luxury travel coverage—they are positioned as a core booking category heading into peak planning season.

Industry context supports that shift. The Global Wellness Institute says the broader wellness economy reached **$6.3 trillion in 2023** and projects it could approach **$9.0 trillion by 2028**. Separately, the World Travel & Tourism Council says travel and tourism contributed **$11.6 trillion** to global GDP in 2025. Together, those datasets suggest that lifestyle travel decisions are increasingly tied to both experience quality and wellness value.

What remains uncertain is whether Hot List visibility translates into sustained occupancy gains across all featured properties, especially as pricing pressure and regional capacity constraints differ sharply by market.
