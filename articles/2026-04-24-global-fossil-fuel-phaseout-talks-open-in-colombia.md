# First Global Fossil-Fuel Phaseout Talks Open in Colombia as COP Frustration Mounts

Delegations from more than 50 countries convened in Colombia on Friday for what multiple outlets described as the first coordinated international talks focused specifically on phasing out fossil fuels, as frustration grows over repeated deadlock in formal UN climate rounds.

## Why this is a desk fit (environment)

This is a core **environment policy-governance** story: the development centers on climate-transition agenda design, fossil-fuel phaseout pathways, and multilateral emissions governance rather than general diplomacy.

## What happened

BBC reported that roughly 60 countries are participating in the Colombia meeting, framing it as a first global effort to move phaseout planning forward outside stalled COP bargaining structures. The Guardian similarly described a “coalition of the willing” trying to bypass petrostate blockages that have slowed progress in recent summits. TIME reported more than 50 participating countries and characterized the gathering as the first international conference dedicated to a fossil-fuel transition roadmap.

## Why it matters

If participating states can align on shared sequencing—such as timelines, financing expectations, and accountability language—the talks could shape negotiating baselines ahead of future COP sessions even without immediate treaty power.

## What is verified vs uncertain

**Verified now:** multiple outlets agree the Colombia meeting is underway and is explicitly focused on phaseout/transition planning for fossil fuels, with participation reported in the 50–60 country range.

**Still uncertain:** whether participants will converge on enforceable milestones, financing mechanisms, or language strong enough to carry into formal UNFCCC negotiations.

## What to watch next

1. Any joint statement language on phaseout timing versus “transition” wording.
2. Whether major producing states join later tracks or remain outside the process.
3. How outcomes are referenced in the next COP preparatory negotiations.
