# Trump Says King Charles Visit Could Help Repair U.S.-UK Relations

U.S. President Donald Trump said a potential visit by King Charles could “absolutely” help repair strained U.S.-UK relations, according to a BBC interview published Friday. The remarks arrive after public criticism of UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer over Middle East policy, putting diplomatic symbolism at the center of a live policy dispute.

BBC reports Trump framed the royal visit as a channel to improve ties. The Independent and Channel News Asia’s syndicated report also describe the same core line: Trump linking a King Charles visit to a possible reset in bilateral relations after recent tensions with Starmer.

## Why this matters

This is a political-signaling story, not just protocol. Public comments from heads of government can alter negotiation tone before formal policy meetings, especially when trade, security, and conflict coordination are already under pressure.

## What to watch next

- Whether Downing Street and the White House align publicly on visit timing and agenda.
- Any follow-on statements that shift from symbolic language to concrete policy commitments.
- Whether rhetoric around Starmer softens in upcoming U.S. and UK briefings.
