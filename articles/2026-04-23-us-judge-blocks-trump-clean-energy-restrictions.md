# Federal Judge Blocks Parts of Trump-Era Slowdown on Wind and Solar Permitting

A federal judge in Massachusetts has blocked parts of the Trump administration's effort to slow federal approvals for wind and solar projects, according to multiple U.S. outlets covering the ruling.

Reports from AP and The Guardian say the injunction pauses a policy that had required high-level political sign-off for certain clean-energy permitting actions. CNBC separately reported the decision as another judicial setback for attempts to stall renewable project approvals.

The ruling does **not** automatically greenlight every contested project, but it removes at least one federal bottleneck while litigation continues.

## What we know

- A federal judge issued an injunction affecting administration actions that had slowed wind and solar project approvals.
- Coverage from AP and The Guardian describes the order as blocking key restrictions tied to federal permitting decisions.
- The legal dispute continues, so additional appeals or narrower follow-on rulings are still possible.

## What remains uncertain

- The exact scope of projects that will see immediate permitting acceleration.
- Whether the administration will win a stay or narrow the injunction on appeal.
- How quickly agencies will operationalize the court order in pending project pipelines.

## Why this fits the environment desk

This story is directly about clean-energy deployment, federal environmental permitting, and the pace of transition infrastructure tied to emissions and land-use outcomes.
