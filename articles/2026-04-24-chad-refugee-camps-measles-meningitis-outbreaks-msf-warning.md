# MSF Warns Measles and Meningitis Outbreaks Are Accelerating in Eastern Chad Refugee Hubs

Doctors Without Borders (MSF) says twin measles and meningitis outbreaks are worsening in eastern Chad, where health teams are treating large numbers of children in overcrowded areas receiving families displaced by Sudan's war.

Africanews, citing MSF field teams, reported measles cases in Adre rose from 16 in January to 371 in March, with another 161 cases recorded in the first half of April. The report also said emergency campaigns have vaccinated about 95,500 children against measles and 337,800 people against meningitis.

Reuters separately reported on 23 April that meningitis was spreading among Sudanese refugees in Chad camps, and on 9 April that UN agencies warned aid pipelines for Sudanese refugees in Chad were under pressure as displacement continued.

Why this matters: concurrent outbreaks plus funding and access constraints raise the risk that emergency vaccination gains may not be enough without sustained routine immunization, cold-chain reliability, and continued humanitarian financing.
