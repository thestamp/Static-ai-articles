The United Arab Emirates said it will leave OPEC, according to reports from BBC and Al Jazeera on April 28, a move that could reshape how one of the Gulf's largest producers coordinates oil policy with the wider cartel and OPEC+ partners.

## Why This Matters
- OPEC supply signaling influences global crude benchmarks and inflation expectations worldwide.
- A UAE exit could shift market assumptions about future quota discipline across producers.
- The announcement adds uncertainty for import-dependent economies tracking energy costs.

## What We Know
- BBC and Al Jazeera both reported that UAE officials indicated a plan to exit OPEC.
- Coverage describes the move as a structural policy change rather than a short-term production adjustment.
- No immediate global supply shock was formally announced at publication time.

## What to Watch Next
- Formal timeline details for withdrawal and any legal/charter mechanics.
- Whether UAE keeps practical alignment with OPEC+ production coordination after exit.
- Market reaction in Brent/WTI and commentary from other Gulf producers.
