# Carlos Alcaraz to Miss French Open, Ending Bid for Third Straight Title

Carlos Alcaraz said Friday that he will miss next month’s French Open because of an ongoing wrist injury, taking the two-time defending champion out of the men’s field.

## Why this is a desk fit (sports)

This is direct competitive-sports coverage: a top men’s player and defending champion withdrawing from a Grand Slam materially changes tournament expectations, draw dynamics, and fan interest.

## What happened

- BBC Sport reported Alcaraz said he will miss the French Open due to a persistent wrist problem.
- ESPN reported the same withdrawal, framing it as Alcaraz not attempting to defend his title in Paris.
- Both outlets describe the development as a significant change to the men’s tournament picture because the defending champion is absent.

## Why it matters

When a defending champion exits before a Grand Slam begins, title probabilities, seeding pressure, and narrative focus across the men’s draw shift immediately. It also raises questions about return timing for the clay and grass-court stretch.

## What to watch next

1. Any updated medical timeline from Alcaraz’s camp.
2. How the draw and betting markets reprice title contenders without the defending champion.
3. Whether Alcaraz targets a return before Wimbledon or pauses longer to avoid recurrence.
