# UK Officials Clash Over Mandelson Vetting Records After Cabinet Testimony

A U.K. political dispute over vetting records around Peter Mandelson intensified after senior Cabinet Office official Cat Little gave evidence that former top civil servant Olly Robbins declined to share a key summary with the department.

BBC coverage said Little outlined procedural and accountability concerns in testimony that has fueled opposition criticism of how the vetting process was handled. The Guardian separately reported Little's account that Robbins refused to provide the summary to the Cabinet Office.

The row has become a broader governance story about who controls sensitive appointment vetting documents and what ministers can review in real time. With parliamentary scrutiny rising, the next phase is likely to center on document disclosure decisions and whether additional officials are called to testify.
