# UK Biobank Suspends Platform Access After China Marketplace Data Listings

UK Biobank has paused access to its research platform after UK officials disclosed that datasets linked to Biobank participants were advertised for sale on Alibaba marketplace listings in China. The incident has shifted immediate focus from scientific output to platform controls, auditability, and cross-border data governance.

In a House of Commons statement, technology minister Ian Murray said Biobank identified three listings, with at least one appearing to include data associated with all 500,000 volunteers. He said the listed datasets did not include names, addresses, or direct contact details, and that the government and platform actions removed listings before confirmed purchases.

A follow-up BBC interview with Biobank chief Sir Rory Collins said institutions tied to the incident were banned and that Biobank was "putting science on hold" while additional safeguards are added. The National Data Guardian separately called for full transparency and accountability to preserve participant trust.

## Why this matters

UK Biobank is a core research infrastructure used globally. Even when datasets are de-identified, incidents involving marketplace listings can reduce participant confidence and pressure organizations to tighten practical controls over extraction, transfer, and downstream handling.

## What to watch next

- The timeline for restoring controlled researcher access after technical safeguards are implemented.
- Findings from Biobank's board-led investigation and the ICO's enquiries.
- Whether UK guidance on research-data controls introduces stricter baseline safeguards for large biomedical datasets.
