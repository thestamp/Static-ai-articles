## Summary
A new European climate assessment says the continent is warming faster than any other region, with heat extremes, glacier loss, and weather volatility intensifying policy pressure ahead of summer risk season.

## What Happened
European climate agencies and the World Meteorological Organization said Europe remained the fastest-warming continent in their latest state-of-climate assessment, citing persistent heat anomalies, retreating glaciers, and compounding weather extremes across multiple subregions.

The report highlights that warming is not uniform: southern and southeastern areas continue to face stronger heat and drought pressure, while other regions are seeing shifts in precipitation and flood risk. Scientists and forecasters say the pattern raises operational risks for energy systems, agriculture, water planning, and urban heat management as the 2026 warm season approaches.

## Why It Matters
This is an environment desk story because it centers on climate-system change and its direct implications for resilience planning, adaptation policy, and near-term hazard preparedness.
