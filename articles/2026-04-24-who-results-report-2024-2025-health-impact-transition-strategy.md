# WHO Reports Measurable 2025 Health Gains Amid Funding Pressure and Strategy Transition

The World Health Organization (WHO) said its newly released **Results Report 2024-2025** shows measurable progress across major global-health targets, even as funding cuts and implementation constraints are beginning to slow delivery in some programs.

In a 23 April news release accompanying the report, WHO said progress was recorded across all three "Triple Billion" outcome areas under its 2019-2025 strategy cycle. The agency cited expanded service coverage for communicable diseases, gains in emergency preparedness, and broader technical support to countries, while noting persistent gaps in areas including diabetes management, measles surveillance, and financial protection.

WHO also said humanitarian operations in 2025 included response activity across dozens of emergencies and countries, framing those operations as part of a broader transition to its next strategy period.

Why this matters: this release is one of WHO's most consequential annual accountability checkpoints, because governments and donors use it to assess whether global health spending is translating into measurable delivery outcomes.
