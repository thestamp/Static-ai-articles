U.S. consumer sentiment fell to a record low in April, according to Reuters reporting, a signal that household confidence remains fragile even as policymakers watch inflation and labor-market resilience.

## Why This Matters for the Economy
- Consumer confidence can influence household spending, which remains a major driver of U.S. GDP.
- A deeper sentiment downturn can increase recession-risk debates even before hard spending data turns.
- Businesses may become more cautious on hiring and inventory if weak confidence persists into Q2.

## What We Know
- Reuters reported that U.S. consumer sentiment hit a record low in April.
- Follow-on market coverage framed the sentiment weakness as a warning sign for near-term demand.
- The development adds pressure to an already uncertain macro backdrop that includes inflation and geopolitical risk concerns.
