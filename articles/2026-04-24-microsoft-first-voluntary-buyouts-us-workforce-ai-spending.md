# Microsoft Launches First-Ever Voluntary Buyout Program for U.S. Staff as AI Spending Climbs

Microsoft is offering voluntary retirement buyouts to eligible U.S.-based employees for the first time, according to CNBC and follow-on reporting from TechCrunch and Yahoo Finance.

The reports say the program could cover roughly 7% of Microsoft’s U.S. workforce and is aimed at employees whose age plus years of service total at least 70, positioning it as a selective, voluntary reduction rather than an immediate broad layoff mandate.

Coverage across outlets frames the move in the context of large ongoing AI infrastructure and model spending, with labor-cost adjustments becoming a visible lever as major tech companies reallocate budgets.

At publication time, the confirmed facts are the launch of the voluntary buyout framework, the reported eligibility threshold, and the cited scale of potential participation. Any final participation totals and team-level impacts remain unconfirmed.
