Southern China is facing elevated flood risk after torrential rain bands pushed across multiple provinces, with some areas forecast to receive 150–200mm of precipitation, according to a Guardian weather-tracker report citing regional meteorological and flood-control alerts.

## Why This Matters
- Extreme rainfall increases near-term risk of flash flooding, landslides, and urban transport disruption.
- Compound climate hazards are emerging simultaneously: flooding risk in East Asia and severe heat in parts of South Asia.
- Local emergency response capacity and early-warning systems are central to limiting casualties and infrastructure damage.

## What We Know
- Heavy rainfall is affecting parts of Guangxi, Guangdong, Fujian, Zhejiang, Jiangxi, and Hunan.
- Reported totals above 100mm are expected in many areas, with localized peaks potentially reaching 150–200mm.
- Flood-control authorities have raised warning posture amid concerns over river and urban drainage stress.

## What to Watch Next
- Province-level emergency notices, evacuation advisories, and school/transport suspensions.
- Verified rainfall totals versus forecast ranges over the next 24–48 hours.
- Any expansion of alerts to downstream river-basin regions.
