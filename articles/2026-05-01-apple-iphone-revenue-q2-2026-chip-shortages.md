# Apple's iPhone revenue jumps to $57 billion despite chip shortages

Apple's March-quarter results indicate iPhone demand remained strong, with revenue reaching $57 billion, according to coverage of the earnings release and company guidance. The topline result suggests Apple's handset business has so far absorbed supply friction better than some analysts expected.

At the same time, Apple and market coverage pointed to ongoing chip constraints that could continue to pressure device availability and component costs in upcoming quarters. The combination of resilient demand and constrained supply keeps investor focus on execution risk heading into the next product cycle.

What remains uncertain: near-term margin effects from component pricing and whether supply bottlenecks materially alter shipment timing in future quarters.
