U.S. regulators said the domestic infant-formula supply tested in a broad new sampling effort appears safe under current federal thresholds, offering a fresh data point for parents and pediatric providers after years of supply and contamination concerns.

### What happened
The Food and Drug Administration said it tested more than 300 infant-formula samples representative of products sold at U.S. retail locations and found contaminant levels below thresholds the agency says would trigger immediate concern. The release frames the effort as the agency’s largest formula-testing sweep to date.

### Why this fits the science-health desk
The core claim is a public-health and pediatric-nutrition safety update from the federal regulator, with direct implications for infant feeding decisions, clinical counseling, and ongoing safety surveillance.

### What to watch next
- Whether FDA publishes additional lot-level or contaminant-trend detail in follow-up updates.
- Any changes to inspection cadence or recall guidance tied to future surveillance rounds.
- Whether pediatric groups adjust public guidance language as new data arrives.
