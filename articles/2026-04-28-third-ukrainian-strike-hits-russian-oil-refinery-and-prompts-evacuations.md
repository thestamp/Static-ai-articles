Ukraine has struck Russia's Tuapse oil refinery for a third time, triggering a large fire and local evacuations, according to BBC reporting corroborated by other international coverage of the same incident timeline.

## Why This Matters
- Repeated strikes on refinery infrastructure raise pressure on Russia's fuel logistics and emergency-response capacity.
- Evacuations indicate localized civilian disruption alongside strategic energy targeting.
- The incident underscores how long-range drone operations are reshaping rear-area risk in the war.

## What We Know
- BBC reported that a third Ukrainian strike hit the Tuapse refinery and prompted evacuations near the facility.
- Reuters and other outlets reported renewed fire activity at the Tuapse refinery consistent with another drone-driven hit.
- Regional authorities described emergency response measures including evacuation actions in nearby areas.

## What to Watch Next
- Independent satellite/fire-monitoring confirmation of damage extent and restoration timelines.
- Whether Russia adjusts refinery protection or fuel-routing after repeated strikes.
- Any official Ukrainian military attribution or additional strike claims in the same corridor.
