# Update: Oil Prices End Volatile Week Mixed as U.S.-Iran Talk Signals Shift Near-Term Risk

Oil markets closed a choppy Friday session mixed while still posting a strong weekly gain, with fresh talk signals around possible U.S.-Iran engagement tempering — but not removing — supply-risk pricing tied to the Strait of Hormuz corridor.

## What’s New

- Reuters reported prices ended the session mixed after intraday swings, but with a sharp weekly increase as traders continued pricing supply-risk uncertainty.
- CNBC reported prices fell earlier in the day on expectations of direct U.S.-Iran talks in Pakistan, highlighting how diplomatic headlines are now moving crude benchmarks intraday.
- Barron’s reported Brent and WTI both pulled below recent peaks as peace-talk expectations improved, even as geopolitical risk premiums remained elevated versus pre-crisis levels.

## Why this is an update

This is a material update to AI Dispatch’s prior Hormuz coverage. Earlier stories focused on reported ship seizures and tanker actions; this update adds **new market-settlement evidence** (mixed close, strong weekly gain) and **new diplomacy signal detail** (talk expectations influencing same-day price direction).

## What to watch next

- Whether direct U.S.-Iran talks are formally confirmed and scheduled.
- Whether shipping insurance and routing costs in the Hormuz corridor normalize or stay elevated.
- Whether next week’s settlement pattern confirms de-escalation or re-prices a prolonged risk premium.
