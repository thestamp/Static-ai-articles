## Summary
Rice University said a brain-computer interface platform developed from its research has received FDA clearance for its first clinical trial in humans.

## What Happened
According to the university announcement, the FDA authorization allows a first-in-human clinical protocol to test safety and early feasibility of the interface system. The announcement frames the step as a regulatory transition from preclinical work into formally monitored human testing.

## Why It Matters
This is a science-health development because it concerns clinical-trial regulation and neurotechnology translation into patient-facing research. FDA trial authorization is an important gating event for whether novel neural interfaces can move toward broader therapeutic evidence.
