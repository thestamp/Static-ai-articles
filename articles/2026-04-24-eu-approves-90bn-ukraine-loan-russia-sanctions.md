# EU Formally Approves €90 Billion Ukraine Loan and New Russia Sanctions

The European Union has formally approved a €90 billion Ukraine support-loan framework and advanced a new sanctions package against Russia, adding fresh policy pressure as the war continues.

Cross-outlet reporting indicates the package combines near-term financial backing for Kyiv with additional economic restrictions aimed at Russian entities. The decision matters because it ties immediate fiscal support to a renewed sanctions cycle at a moment when financing durability and enforcement sequencing remain central to the conflict’s trajectory.

For global diplomacy, the move is significant as a bloc-level signal of sustained EU alignment on both funding and punitive measures, even as member states continue to debate implementation pace and downstream market effects.
