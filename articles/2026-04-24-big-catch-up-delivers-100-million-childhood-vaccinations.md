# WHO-Backed Big Catch-Up Delivers 100 Million+ Childhood Vaccine Doses Across 36 Countries

A WHO-UNICEF-Gavi campaign launched to recover routine immunization losses after the COVID-19 shock has delivered more than 100 million childhood vaccine doses since 2023, according to statements from the agencies and Reuters reporting.

The initiative, called **The Big Catch-Up**, reached an estimated 18.3 million children ages 1-5 across 36 participating countries between 2023 and 2025. WHO and Gavi said around 12.3 million of those children were previously "zero-dose" (no prior routine vaccination), while about 15 million had not received a measles shot before the campaign.

Health agencies framed the campaign as a major recovery step, but warned that mass catch-up drives are resource-intensive and cannot replace stable routine immunization systems. WHO said 14.3 million infants globally still missed their first routine vaccine dose in 2024, and that measles outbreaks continue to rise in multiple regions.

Why this matters: the campaign's scale shows that coordinated emergency immunization pushes can recover missed cohorts, but global health agencies are signaling that long-term outbreak prevention still depends on rebuilding routine, year-round primary vaccination coverage.
