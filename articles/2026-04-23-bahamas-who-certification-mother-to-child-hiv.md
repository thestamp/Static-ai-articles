# WHO Certifies The Bahamas for Eliminating Mother-to-Child HIV Transmission

The World Health Organization (WHO) and the Pan American Health Organization (PAHO) said on April 22 that **The Bahamas has been certified for eliminating mother-to-child transmission (MTCT) of HIV**, marking a major regional public-health milestone.

In a follow-up statement, UNAIDS said the certification also reflects elimination of mother-to-child transmission of hepatitis B in The Bahamas, and framed the outcome as evidence that sustained antenatal screening and treatment programs can drive transmission toward elimination targets.

Why this matters: EMTCT certification is a high-bar validation process, and health officials often use it as a signal for where maternal and child health delivery systems are consistently reaching pregnant patients with testing, prevention, and follow-up care.
