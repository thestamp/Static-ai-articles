Bank of England Deputy Governor Sarah Breeden said global equity markets appear priced for too little risk, warning that multiple shocks could force a repricing, according to BBC reporting on April 24. The warning comes as major indexes remain near highs and policymakers watch for fragilities in private credit and broader non-bank finance.

## Why This Matters for the Economy
- A sharp equity repricing can tighten financial conditions, raise corporate funding costs, and slow hiring and investment.
- UK and global policymakers have increasingly focused on non-bank financial vulnerabilities, especially private-credit interconnections.
- If risk assets and credit reset together, spillovers could move quickly from markets into the real economy.

## What We Know
- Breeden said asset prices look high relative to the range of macro and financial-stability risks now in play.
- The Bank of England has been tracking systemic risk channels beyond traditional banks, including market-based finance.
- No timing or magnitude for a market adjustment was provided; this is a risk signal, not a point forecast.
